2008，2009年国内轿车市场部分品牌份额示意图

When people buy a private car, they always consider many factors, such as price, performance, appearance, fuel consumption and so on, and finally choose the most suitable car. Now we can see some data in the picture. In 2008, domestic brands accounted for 25% of the market, Japanese 35% and American 15%. In 2009, American brands remained unchanged, but the share of domestic and Japanese brands was almost reversed. The share of domestic brands exceeded 30% and nearly 35%, while Japanese dropped to 25%. According to this, we can conclude that some key factors have changed.

Japanese brands mainly sell mid-range and entry-level cars in our market, which overlaps with that of domestic brands. They are still in their initial stage of development. When domestic vehicles are insufficient in manufacturing technique and performance, consumers in this range will tend to choose Japanese brands’ cars. These are the reasons why the Japanese brands’ share was higher than that of these domestic‘s.

In 2009, when the performance of domestic brand vehicles is basically the same as Japanese brands’ products, price will become a decisive factor in market shares. The import of Japanese brands’ vehicles needs to pay tariff, therefore the price is bound to be higher than that of domestic brands’ vehicles on the premise of similar costs. As a result, the market share of domestic brands increases and exceeds that of Japanese brands.
